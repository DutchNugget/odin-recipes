Authentic Gazpacho

Ingredients

Vegetables:

  • 6 ripe tomatoes, peeled and chopped
  • 1 cucumber, peeled and diced
  • 1 red bell pepper, chopped
  • 1 small red onion, finely chopped
  • 1 garlic clove, minced

Liquids:

  • 3 cups cold water (or vegetable broth)
  • 3 tbsp extra virgin olive oil
  • 2 tbsp red wine vinegar
  • 1 tbsp sherry vinegar (optional, for extra depth of flavor)

Seasonings:

  • 1 tsp sea salt (to taste)
  • 1/4 tsp black pepper (freshly ground)
  • A pinch of cumin (optional, for a warm note)

Garnishes (optional):

  • Fresh parsley or basil, chopped
  • Croutons or toasted bread cubes
  • A drizzle of olive oil
  • Chopped veggies (like cucumber or bell pepper) for texture

Description

Gazpacho is a refreshing and vibrant Spanish cold soup that’s perfect for hot summer days. Packed with fresh vegetables like tomatoes, cucumbers, peppers, and onions, it’s a burst of flavor in every spoonful. The soup is typically blended to a smooth consistency, though some like to keep it a bit chunky for added texture. A drizzle of olive oil, a splash of vinegar, and a pinch of salt tie everything together, making it a zesty, cooling dish. It’s light, healthy, and full of antioxidants—perfect as a starter, snack, or even a light meal. Serve it chilled with a sprinkle of fresh herbs, and maybe a side of crusty bread for the ultimate summer treat!

Instructions

  1. Prepare the vegetables:
    Start by peeling and chopping the tomatoes, cucumber, red bell pepper, and red onion. Finely mince the garlic clove.

  2. Combine the ingredients:
    In a large blender or food processor, add the chopped tomatoes, cucumber, bell pepper, onion, and garlic. Add the cold water (or vegetable broth) to the mixture.

  3. Blend until smooth:
    Pulse the mixture until it's completely smooth. You can adjust the consistency by adding a little more water or broth if needed.

  4. Season the gazpacho:
    Add the olive oil, red wine vinegar, sherry vinegar (if using), sea salt, black pepper, and a pinch of cumin (if desired). Blend again until everything is well combined.

  5. Chill the soup:
    Transfer the gazpacho to a large bowl or container and refrigerate for at least 2 hours, or preferably overnight, for the flavors to meld and the soup to chill.

  6. Serve:

When ready to serve, stir the soup gently and adjust seasoning if needed. Ladle into bowls or pour in a glass and garnish with fresh parsley or basil, croutons, extra chopped vegetables, and a drizzle of olive oil for extra flavor.
