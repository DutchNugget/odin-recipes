Nasi Goreng

Description

Nasi Goreng is a beloved Indonesian fried rice, known for its rich, caramelized brown color and bold flavors. Traditionally topped with a fried egg, this dish is incredibly simple to make, with no need for hard-to-find ingredients. It's one of my all-time favorite Indonesian dishes, and I’m sure it’ll become one of yours too!

Ingredients

Chicken

  • 1 tbsp oil
  • 5 oz / 150g chicken breast, thinly sliced (or other protein)
  • 1 tbsp kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)

Rice

  • 1.5 tbsp oil
  • 2 garlic cloves, finely chopped
  • 1 tsp red chili, finely chopped
  • 1 small onion, diced
  • 3 cups cooked white rice, day old and cold
  • 2 tbsp kecap manis (sweet soy sauce)
  • 2 tsp shrimp paste, optional

Garnishes / Side Servings (Optional)

  • 4 eggs, fried to taste
  • 1 green onion, sliced
  • Tomatoes and cucumbers, cut into wedges/chunks
  • Fried shallots, store-bought (optional)
  • Lime wedges

Instructions

  1. Heat the oil:
    In a large skillet or wok, heat the oil over high heat.

  2. Stir-fry the aromatics:
    Add the chopped chili and garlic. Stir-fry for about 10 seconds until fragrant.

  3. Cook the onion:
    Toss in the diced onion and sauté for 1 minute until softened.

  4. Cook the chicken:
    Add the chicken and cook until it’s mostly white. Stir in 1 tbsp of kecap manis and continue cooking for another minute, or until the chicken is cooked through and lightly caramelised.

  5. Fry the rice:
    Add the cold, day-old rice to the skillet. Stir in 2 tbsp of kecap manis and optional shrimp paste. Keep stirring for about 2 minutes, allowing the sauce to reduce and the rice to caramelise – this step is key for flavour!

  6. Serve:
    Once the rice is nicely fried, transfer to a plate and garnish with your choice of toppings like sliced green onions, extra chili, or crispy fried shallots.

Serving

  1. As a side:
    Serve alongside your favorite Asian dishes or as part of a big spread. It pairs well with a variety of main dishes!

  2. Traditional Indonesian style:
    Make it a full meal by topping with a fried egg (sunny-side-up with a runny yolk!) and adding slices of tomato and cucumber on the side.
